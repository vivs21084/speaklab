What this is, in three lines

You speak on a real topic, the app shows you where your sentences ramble ("then... then..."), you plan a better structure, and you say it again — shorter. That re-take is where your English improves; research calls it the 4/3/2 method. Five new phrases a day get drilled into your own sentences, so your vocabulary grows in speech, not on paper.

One-time setup

Laptop (main gym)

  1. Open the app in Chrome.
  2. Click 🎙 Record once — Chrome asks for the microphone → Allow.
  3. Open your personal topics link once (Claude gave it to you — it's in Outputs/Spoken-English/my-topics-link.txt). You'll see "✓ 42 personal topics loaded". Now the app suggests YOUR projects, not generic topics.

iPhone

  1. Open the same personal topics link in Safari (copy it from the Files app → Claude Cowork → Outputs → Spoken-English → my-topics-link.txt).
  2. Tap Share → Add to Home Screen. Allow the mic when you first record.
💡 History stays on each device separately. Do your Sunday review on the laptop.

Step 5 — Plan, then speak again (Attempt 2)

The Review phase tells you exactly what to do: say your map out loud ("There are three parts: ..."), pick your signposts, rebuild your two worst sentences. Then record the SAME topic, one minute shorter. The app compares automatically:

Step 6 — Get AI coaching, save it

Press 🎧 AI analyse my AUDIO (best — sends your actual recording: accurate transcript with your real "um"s, plus coaching) or 🤖 Get AI feedback (uses the text in the box). Either way: your two worst sentences rebuilt (Original → Cleaner → Advanced), phrases for that exact topic, and a restructured version to say aloud before Attempt 2. Needs sign-in. Then click Save attempt to history — "☁ synced" means it's in your cloud history on every device.

The 5 daily phrases (the "1,000 phrases" method)

What you'll see
The key risk here is …, because …💼 work ✓ used 2×
Let me walk you through the trade-offs.💼 work ✓ used 0×
Long story short, …☕ everyday ✓ used 1×

Every day you get 5 phrases (3 for your mode, 2 from the other). The rule that makes them stick: say each one inside your own sentence about your real life or work, out loud — not just read it. Tick ✓ when you use one in a recording; least-used phrases come back sooner. The library starts at 185 curated phrases and grows toward 1,000 — this drip-feed with forced use is what the research says works, mass recitation is what it says doesn't.

Troubleshooting

  • "Transcription stopped" warning: your audio is still recording. Finish speaking, save the audio, and type/paste a rough transcript — or send the audio file to Claude to transcribe.
  • Transcript garbles your words: check the accent model is English (India); fix key words in the box before analysing — the scores only need the structure words to be right.
  • Closed the tab mid-session: reopen the app — it restores your unsaved transcript automatically.
  • Phone has no history from laptop: normal — each device keeps its own. Export JSON from the laptop monthly as backup.
  • Topics say "generic": you haven't opened your personal topics link on this device yet.
